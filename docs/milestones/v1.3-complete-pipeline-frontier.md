# Milestone 1.3 — Bounded complete-pipeline frontier

Date: 2026-07-24

Status: accepted draft-project baseline after Phase 10 evidence.

## Decision

Promote a width-four repair-potential frontier to the canonical browser production path. The exact Phase 9 single-candidate path remains frontier member zero and is available as an explicit rollback.

The accepted boundary retains already-valid construction candidates after exact clue allocation and construction-level victim repair, then runs the full downstream repair and editorial chain independently for each retained finalist.

## Retained frontier

The construction frontier preserves member zero and retains at most four non-dominated candidates using complete metrics plus downstream repair-potential topology:

```text
minimize residual panels and panel regions
minimize isolated panels
maximize residual concentration
maximize letter cells, crossings, answers and external clue capacity
minimize weak fill and clue-text cells
```

A small current panel disadvantage may survive only when another measured dimension prevents dominance. Width truncation is deterministic and fully recorded.

## Complete finalist chain

```text
construction frontier
-> portfolio polish
-> clue-footprint repack
-> adaptive clue repack
-> clue-tail absorption
-> single-footprint reflow
-> pair-footprint reflow
-> targeted residual-victim repair
-> shared exact legacy guard
-> same-geometry editorial repair
-> complete validation
-> canonical final comparison
```

The historical legacy guard is generated once and cloned per finalist. The frontier does not multiply unrestricted construction attempts or legacy generation.

## Preserved negative evidence

Runs `30040602957` and `30041156501` accidentally omitted the locked browser-equivalent environment and exercised legacy construction on both A/B sides. They are preserved as a benchmark-harness defect, not as algorithm evidence.

## Accepted limitations

- Exact clue allocation still occurs before frontier retention; Phase 10 does not claim allocation-cost savings.
- The frontier improves the canonical structural objective but may accept disclosed lower-priority editorial trade-offs.
- Zero-panel generation is not guaranteed.
- Clue prose remains truthful but is not publication edited.
- Full-corpus retrieval, clue-feasibility ranking and partial-state beam search remain non-default research features.
